Bahubali 2

2017 Indian epic fantasy action film directed by S. S. Rajamouli

The movie Baahubali 2: The Conclusion, directed by S. S. Rajamouli and starring Prabhas, was made with an estimated budget of around ₹250 crore, making it one of the most expensive Indian films of its time. The film became a historic blockbuster and earned approximately ₹1,788–1,810 crore worldwide gross, setting several box-office records and becoming the highest-grossing Indian film for many years. It crossed ₹1,000 crore globally within just 10 days and performed exceptionally well in India as well as overseas markets such as the USA, UAE, Australia, and the UK.

In terms of collections across Indian states and regions, the film recorded extraordinary numbers. The combined Andhra Pradesh and Telangana (APTG) region contributed around ₹141.7 crore gross in its initial run, while Karnataka contributed about ₹53 crore, Tamil Nadu around ₹52 crore, and Kerala approximately ₹28.5 crore. The remaining Indian regions together added nearly ₹286.8 crore. The Hindi version was also a massive success and played a major role in turning the film into a true pan-India phenomenon. The movie's worldwide success helped establish Telugu cinema on a global scale and made the question “Why did Kattappa kill Baahubali?” one of the most famous movie discussions in Indian cinema history.

RRR

RRR is a 2022 Indian epic action-drama film directed by S. S. Rajamouli

The film stars N. T. Rama Rao Jr. as Komaram Bheem and Ram Charan as Alluri Sitarama Raju, with supporting roles played by Alia Bhatt and Ajay Devgn. Set during British colonial rule in the 1920s, the movie presents a fictional story imagining a friendship between two legendary Indian freedom fighters, Komaram Bheem and Alluri Sitarama Raju, before they became famous revolutionaries. The film is known for its grand scale, breathtaking action sequences, emotional storytelling, and powerful visual effects.

Made on an estimated budget of around ₹550 crore, RRR was one of the most expensive Indian films ever produced at the time of its release. The movie became a massive commercial success, grossing approximately ₹1,250–1,390 crore worldwide, making it one of the highest-grossing Indian films in history. The film received worldwide acclaim for its direction, performances, music, and action sequences. Its song "Naatu Naatu", composed by M. M. Keeravani, created a global sensation and won the Academy Award (Oscar) for Best Original Song, becoming the first song from an Indian film to achieve this honor. RRR played a significant role in increasing the international recognition of Telugu cinema and is regarded as one of the most influential Indian films of the 21st century.

Kalki 2898 AD

Kalki 2898 AD is a 2024 Indian Telugu-language epic mythological science fiction film[6][7] written and directed by Nag Ashwin. Produced by Vyjayanthi Movies.

Kalki is a 2024 Indian epic science-fiction film directed by Nag Ashwin and produced by . The film stars Prabhas in the lead role, alongside Deepika Padukone, Amitabh Bachchan, Kamal Haasan, and Disha Patani. Set in the year 2898 AD, the movie combines Indian mythology with futuristic science fiction and revolves around the prophesied arrival of Kalki, the final avatar of Lord Vishnu, who is destined to restore balance and defeat evil. The story takes place in a dystopian future where resources are scarce and humanity is controlled by powerful forces.

Made on an estimated budget of around ₹600 crore, Kalki 2898 AD is among the most expensive Indian films ever produced. The film received praise for its visual effects, world-building, action sequences, and performances, particularly those of Amitabh Bachchan and Prabhas. It became a major box-office success, grossing over ₹1,000 crore worldwide, making it one of the highest-grossing Indian films of all time. The movie's unique blend of ancient Indian mythology and futuristic technology, along with its grand scale and cinematic visuals, helped it gain widespread popularity in India and international markets. It is also planned as the first installment of a larger cinematic universe.

Pushpa - The Rise

Pushpa is an Indian Telugu-language action drama film series created by Sukumar and produced by Mythri Movie Makers, Muttamsetty Media and Sukumar Writings.

Pushpa: The Rise is a 2021 Telugu-language action drama film directed by Sukumar. The story follows Pushpa Raj, a daily wage laborer who gradually rises to become a powerful figure in the red sandalwood smuggling business operating in the forests of Andhra Pradesh. The film was made with a budget of approximately ₹170–200 crore and went on to collect more than ₹360 crore worldwide, making it one of the biggest commercial successes of 2021. Known for its gripping storyline, action sequences, memorable dialogues, and popular songs such as "Srivalli" and "Oo Antava Oo Oo Antava," the movie became a nationwide phenomenon and laid the foundation for its sequel, Pushpa 2: The Rule.

Salaar

Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire is a 2023 Telugu-language action thriller film directed by Prashanth Neel. The story revolves around Deva, a mysterious and powerful man who is drawn back into a violent conflict to help his childhood friend Vardharaja Mannaar secure his rightful place in the fictional kingdom of Khansaar. The film is known for its intense action sequences, large-scale world-building, and emotional friendship at its core. Made on an estimated budget of around ₹270 crore, Salaar became a major box-office success, grossing over ₹700 crore worldwide. The movie received praise for its action, background score, cinematography, and high-energy storytelling, and it concludes by setting up the sequel, Salaar: Part 2 – Shouryaanga Parvam.
